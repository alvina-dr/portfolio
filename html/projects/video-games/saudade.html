The pitch

Link to my initial pitch for the administration.



The story of Saudade was heavily inspired by my grandfather's life. He grew up in Portugal, but had to come to France at the age of 17. That's also where the name of the game "Saudade" comes from. It's a portuguese word that's not easily translated in french but means something along the lines of : "Deep nostalgic longing for someone or something absent, infused with a melancholic awareness that it may never return".

This story is about having to leave your own country and miss it from afar. But it's also about paving your own way, tracing your path - just like the player will do during the game - and missing home / thinking about what home is to you.

Actual game

The team decided to lean into this direction and actually make the game take place in Portugal. There was a heavy research work done on the post Second World War period (during which Portugal was under a dictatorship).

So the game is actually about Ernestina, an old lady who returns to her childhood memories in Portugal. Each memory is a loop that lasts a few minutes during which the player will re-explore Ernestina's past and her native country. They will need to interact and modify the environment to make their next loop more efficient. "Awaken buried moments, nostalgic emotions, and forgotten secrets to find the person you were".
